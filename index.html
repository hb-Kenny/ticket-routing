Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from the support team about ticket routing, forms, and escalations.

Tier 1 — General support. Initial response and resolution within 2 business days. Covers login help, basic questions, referral creation, catalog requests, and provider changes.

Tier 2 — Specialized support. Resolution within 3–5 business days. Covers refunds, account issues, declinations, and reimbursements.

Tier 3 — Product/system investigations. No committed resolution timeline — periodic updates are provided until findings are delivered. Covers system issues, bugs, and product investigations.

Tier 4 — Engineering support. No committed timeline, handled based on priority. Only applicable to Product team escalations requiring dev involvement.

Most Gov plan members (Aetna, Alignment, BCBS SC, Elderplan, HAP, Isaac Health, Longevity, Network Health, Simpra, UHC, Verda, etc.) use the Gov Member Support Form specific to their plan. Each client has its own Zendesk subdomain.

Use the "What form do I use?" tool on the Overview page to find the exact form and direct link for each client.

Customer Service handles general member inquiries — login help, order/service questions, refunds, referral creation, registration, and member communications.

RBC handles anything provider or member assignment related — provider change requests, declinations, 2hr show-up fees, parking requests, meal delivery questions, and member re-assignments.

If unsure, ask: is the issue about the member's account/service experience? → Customer Service. Is it about a provider or care coordination? → RBC.

Route to Product (Tier 3) when the issue involves:

  • A system outage or error affecting multiple members
  • Funds not appearing in a member's account
  • Consumer portal cart or transaction issues
  • Bugs or system limitations preventing normal operations
  • McKesson product issues

If it's a one-off member issue that can be resolved manually, it likely belongs to Customer Service or RBC first.

THB has three internal-only forms accessible from the Zendesk Links page under the 🔒 Internal Only filter:

  • THB Product Form — Used to report product bugs, system issues, or request product investigations.
  • THB Marketing Form — Used for internal marketing requests and communications.
  • THB Member Support Form — Used internally for member support escalations.

These forms are not shared externally with members or case managers.

These are Humana-specific internal forms used by Case Managers and Leadership only — not for members directly.

  • Humana C3A — Referral updates, item exchanges, lost checks, emergency referrals, and account changes for C3A members.
  • Humana PHCS — Member service questions, referral address changes, provider issues, and account changes for PHCS members.
  • Humana Medicaid — Meals issues, item exchanges, referral updates, and account changes for Medicaid members.

Each team can escalate up through the tiers as needed. The standard path for all teams is Tier 1 → Tier 2 → Tier 3. Product tickets can escalate further to Tier 4 (Engineering) if dev work is required.

To escalate, update the ticket's Support Tier field in Zendesk and reassign to the appropriate group. Add a note summarizing what's been attempted and why escalation is needed.

All member grievances go to the Compliance team (Tier 2). Use the Grievances issue type on the member's form.

For UHP specifically, there is a dedicated UHP Grievance Support Form — this is Leadership only and routes directly to Governance & Risk Management at Tier 4.

Service Level Agreements & Ticket Tiers

Tier 1 General Support
Initial Response 1 business day
Resolution 2 business days
Login help, general questions, catalog requests, provider changes, referral creation.
Tier 2 Specialized Support
Initial Response 1 business day
Resolution 3–5 business days
Refunds, account issues, declinations, reimbursements, member re-assignments.
Tier 3 Product Support
Initial Response 1 business day
Resolution No committed timeline
System issues, bugs, product investigations. Periodic updates provided throughout. Requires Ops Lead approval + documentation.
Tier 4 Engineering Support
Initial Response 1 business day
Resolution Priority-based
Engineering investigations and dev work escalated from Product Support. No committed timeline.

📌 Important Notes

  • All response and resolution times are calculated in business days
  • Tier 3 and Tier 4 tickets involve complex investigations and engineering work
  • Higher tier tickets receive periodic status updates throughout the investigation process
  • Initial response SLA applies to first meaningful contact with the ticket requester
🚨 Policy Update

Tier 3 Escalation Requirements

Effective immediately — all agents must follow the updated process when escalating tickets to Product Support (Tier 3).

What Changed

  • Only Ops Leads / Managers may escalate to Tier 3.
  • Tickets will only be routed to Product Support when required internal documentation is present and explicitly confirmed.
  • Tickets that arrive at Tier 3 without the required documentation will be returned to the updater at Tier 2.

Required Steps to Escalate to Tier 3

Before changing the Support Tier or assigning a ticket to Product Support, complete all of the following:

1
Add complete internal documentation — required before any escalation.
2
Add an internal/private note that includes:
  • Troubleshooting steps performed and results
  • Reproduction steps and environment details (if applicable)
  • Logs, screenshots, or attachments
  • End-user identifying information (MRN, tenant, contact, etc.)
  • Clear escalation justification — why Tier 3 is required
3
Confirm the documentation — check the field "Internal Documentation Provided for Escalation to Product Support". The ticket will only escalate when this checkbox is marked.
⚠️ Do not manually set Group → Product Support unless the documentation is already present and the confirmation checkbox is checked.

What Happens if Requirements Are Not Met

If a ticket is escalated to Tier 3 without the required documentation, the system will automatically:

  • Return the ticket to the updater (reassigned to the agent who attempted the escalation)
  • Tag the ticket missing_documentation & add_trouble_shooting_steps
  • Add an internal note describing what's missing and what actions are required
  • Product Support will not receive or work the ticket until documentation is provided

How to Remediate a Returned Ticket

1
Read the internal note that explains what's missing.
2
Add the required documentation (internal note) and attach supporting files.
3
Check "Internal Documentation Provided for Escalation to Product Support".
4
The ticket will automatically re-route to Product Support and the tags will be cleared.
