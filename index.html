🖥️ THB User System Issues

For internal THB staff experiencing system issues — follow this process before escalating.

  1. Submit a Zendesk ticket and expect initial movement within 3 business days (movement = documented acknowledgment, triage, or next steps — not necessarily resolution).
  2. If there is no movement within that window, escalate to your manager.
  3. If a manager is escalating, do so in the #product Slack channel — link the ticket and tag @Kenny, @Ashley & @Kassandra. Please do not tag or DM individual PMs directly.
  4. If 3 business days isn't acceptable due to urgency, leaders should determine whether the issue warrants a Type 1 escalation.

💡 Product Enhancements & Requests Leadership

For requesting new features or product improvements — use Jira Product Discovery.

  1. Create an Idea in Jira Product Discovery.
  2. Monitor the Intake & Prioritization board in Jira Product Discovery for status updates.
  3. Accounts/Ops leadership will clarify prioritization in the weekly Intake meeting with the Product team.

💳 Lynx Fund Adjustment Workflow
Rollovers · Refunds · Fund Adjustments
▼
⚠️ Account Manager Approval Required
  • Confirm approval is documented before proceeding.
  • If not documented, contact the Account Manager assigned to the Health Plan.
  • Use the MA Account Manager HP List below to locate the correct AM.
  • If the ticket came from the HP rep: Abbey Novak — this counts as Account approval.
✅ Before Processing Any Adjustment
  • Verify the member's demographics in HomeAlign (HA).
  • Confirm the type of purchase (OTC, Grocery, Rent Reimbursement, etc.).
  • Always verify the purchase type in HA, even if documented in the ticket.
1
Verify Member & Purchase Info in HomeAlign
  • Copy the MRN from the ZD ticket and search in the Flex HP tenant.
  • Open an active Payor Authorization to confirm demographics.
  • Confirm the exact adjustment amount and Client Program Name (Wallet/Purse: OTC, Grocery, Rent, etc.).
  • Note: a single referral may have multiple items — only specific items may need adjustment.
2
Locate the Member & Correct Account in Lynx
  • Log in, select the correct Tenant, then search by member name (no leading/trailing spaces).
  • Verify demographics match HomeAlign before proceeding.
  • Go to the Accounts Tab — use the toggle to show active-only or all accounts.
  • Locate the correct Product Code for the Client Program. Use the Flexcard Lynx 2026 Product Code reference in Notion to decode it.
  • Confirm the account is active and the date range aligns with the benefit period.
3
Complete the Fund Adjustment in Lynx
  • Click the three dots (⋯) under Actions on the correct account line → Adjust Account.
  • Enter the amount, select "Missed Funding" as the adjustment type.
  • Add a note with your initials: reason + ZD ticket number (e.g., Request for Refund — ZD#123456-KBB).
  • Leave Client Reference Identifier blank. Click Submit.
  • Refresh the page to confirm the balance updated.
CAP Reminder: Some accounts have a funding cap. If the full refund can't be applied in one transaction, process it in multiple smaller amounts. If it still won't apply, escalate to Product.
4
Close the Referral in HomeAlign (if applicable)
  • Ensure the refund is fully submitted in Lynx first.
  • In HomeAlign, navigate to Referrals/Cases → search by MRN or Referral ID (check "Include inactive referrals").
  • Change the status to Closed, enter a closure note (e.g., Closed and refunded referral #XXXX associated w/ZD#123456), and save.
  • Verify the referral shows Closed in the search view.
5
Update & Close the ZD Ticket
  • Leave an internal note documenting steps taken.
  • Apply the "Funds Adjustment" macro for the public reply — update/remove sections as needed for the specific request.
  • Solve the ticket.

2
Access & Fill Out the Catalog Request Spreadsheet

💼 Working ZD Tickets — Job Aid
Viewing · Transferring · Notes · Macros · Followers · Mass Updates
▼
⚠️ Always remember
  • Read the ticket thoroughly before taking action.
  • Acknowledge receipt and communicate next steps to the submitter.
  • Changes do not auto-save — always click Submit.
1
Access Zendesk
  • Navigate to thehelperbees.zendesk.com and log in with your THB email & password.
  • Home Dashboard: view, sort, and open tickets directly. Views Dashboard: filter by status and export.
2
Check "Following" and "CC'd" Tickets
  • These are not in your bucket but require your team's response.
  • Once responded, Unfollow the ticket to clear it from your queue.
3
Transferring a Ticket to Another Team
  • Change the Assignee. Icon = group, no icon = individual user.
  • Internal note is required — include investigation details and a direct ask.
  • If a callback was requested, assign to a team that does outbound calls with full call details.
  • Submit in Open status — Pending means the receiving team won't see it needs action.
  • Do not close another team's ticket. If tagged, respond and submit as Open. Only the assigned team marks it Solved.
4
Internal Note vs Public Reply
  • Internal Note — THB teams only. Use for investigation context, transfer notes, escalation details.
  • Public Reply — visible to all external parties. Use for member-facing communications.
5
Applying a Macro
  • Select "Apply Macro" at the bottom left of the ticket.
  • Find the appropriate macro (it populates a canned response in the comment field).
  • Press Enter to apply — always read the response carefully before submitting.
6
Tagging Another Person or Team as Follower
  • Add them as a Follower, switch to Internal Note, then tag the person/team.
  • Press Enter to input, then submit in the appropriate status.
  • Include full investigation details — they need enough context to act immediately.
7
Mass Updating Tickets
  • Select tickets using the checkboxes on the left, then click Edit at the bottom.
  • Make updates (assign team, apply macros, etc.), then click Submit to apply to all selected.
8
BCBS AZ Form Tickets — Required Checkboxes
  • Check "Member contact needed" or "Member contacted" when applicable.
  • Required: "Member contacted" must be checked before closing any ticket that was marked as contact needed.

💸 Payor Auth Refund — HomeAlign
Refunding dollars/points via a manual allocation adjustment
▼
1
Copy the Member ID from the ZD Ticket
  • Open the Zendesk ticket containing the refund request and copy the Member ID (Ctrl+C / Cmd+C).
2
Log into HomeAlign & Navigate to the Correct Tenant
  • Identify the correct tenant based on the member's plan (noted in the ZD ticket) and select it from the dashboard.
3
Search for the Member via Payor Authorization
  • Click the Payor Authorization tab in the tenant dashboard.
  • Paste the Member ID into the "Member #" field and click Search.
  • Click the member's Payor Auth ID hyperlink (or right-click → Open in new tab).
4
Access Auth Dollars
  • On the Payor Authorization page, select the "Auth Dollars" tab (right-side menu).
  • Click the hyperlinked year (e.g., 2025) under the Dates column in the pop-up.
5
Add a Manual Allocation Adjustment
  • Click "Add manual allocation adjustment" (blue button on the right).
  • Enter the refund amount in the Dollars Available field.
  • Toggle on "Non-invoiceable".
  • In Transaction Notes, explain the reason and include the ZD ticket link.
  • Click "Add transaction" and confirm the Dollars Available balance updated correctly.
6
Confirm & Close the ZD Ticket
  • Return to the ZD ticket and reply confirming the refund has been processed, including a link to the member's Payor Authorization.
  • Mark the ticket as Solved.

💰 Member Refunds — Scenarios & Routing
Unfulfilled referrals · Misbilled referrals · Contacts & turnaround
▼

Scenario 1 — Unfulfilled Referral

Referral has not been invoiced or services have not been completed. Member requests refund of points or dollars (e.g., changed mind or no providers available).

Handling

  • Follow the How To Handle Refund Requests guide in Notion.
  • Care Concierge can provide further assistance.

Scenario 2 — Misbilled Referral

Provider invoiced for services not rendered or cannot prove service completion. Handled by Compliance.

Handling

  • Points Programs: Refund issued as new, non-invoiceable referral. Member selects matching services. Cannot combine with existing points.
  • Encounter Programs: Refund issued per encounter (e.g., 5 misbilled = 5 refunded).

Routing & Contacts

  • Customer Service: create "Audits – Targeted" tickets and assign to Compliance.
  • Product Support: redirect or create these tickets as needed.
  • Audits performed by: Iliana, Shani, and Laurie. For status updates, contact them directly.
  • Iliana is handling refunds for Humana and Centene programs.
  • Turnaround: typically 7–10 business days (may vary by provider responsiveness).

🔒 Lost or Stolen Card

1
Locate the member in the Client Admin Portal and click the Member ID.
2
Click the Cards tab on the Member Details page.
3
Click the ellipsis (⋯) on the correct card → "Report Lost/Stolen". This freezes the card and stops all transactions.
4
When the confirmation pop-up appears, click "Accept."
5
Ask if the member wants a replacement card. If No → end. If Yes → continue.
6
Click "Reissue Card". Inform the member it will be mailed to the primary address on file.
7
Choose Standard or Rush delivery. Inform member standard delivery is up to 14 days.
8
Ask about disputed transactions. If none → end. If yes → warm transfer to dispute the transactions.

📬 Member Never Received Debit Card

1
Locate the member in the Client Admin Portal and click the Member ID.
2
Go to the Cards tab and locate the card's Creation Date.
3
Check the Creation Date:
  • More than 14 days ago: Click ⋯ → "Report Lost/Stolen" and follow the lost card process to reissue.
  • Less than 14 days ago: Advise the member that standard delivery takes up to 14 days. Ask them to call back after the window if still not received.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from the support team about ticket routing, forms, and escalations.

Tier 1 — General support. Initial response and resolution within 2 business days. Covers login help, basic questions, referral creation, catalog requests, and provider changes.

Tier 2 — Specialized support. Resolution within 3–5 business days. Covers refunds, account issues, declinations, and reimbursements.

Tier 3 — Product/system investigations. No committed resolution timeline — periodic updates are provided until findings are delivered. Covers system issues, bugs, and product investigations.

Tier 4 — Engineering support. No committed timeline, handled based on priority. Only applicable to Product team escalations requiring dev involvement.

Most Gov plan members (Aetna, Alignment, BCBS SC, Elderplan, HAP, Isaac Health, Longevity, Network Health, Simpra, UHC, Verda, etc.) use the Gov Member Support Form specific to their plan. Each client has its own Zendesk subdomain.

Use the "What form do I use?" tool on the Overview page to find the exact form and direct link for each client.

Customer Service handles general member inquiries — login help, order/service questions, refunds, referral creation, registration, and member communications.

RBC handles anything provider or member assignment related — provider change requests, declinations, 2hr show-up fees, parking requests, meal delivery questions, and member re-assignments.

If unsure, ask: is the issue about the member's account/service experience? → Customer Service. Is it about a provider or care coordination? → RBC.

Route to Product (Tier 3) when the issue involves:

  • A system outage or error affecting multiple members
  • Funds not appearing in a member's account
  • Consumer portal cart or transaction issues
  • Bugs or system limitations preventing normal operations
  • McKesson product issues

If it's a one-off member issue that can be resolved manually, it likely belongs to Customer Service or RBC first.

THB has three internal-only forms accessible from the Zendesk Links page under the 🔒 Internal Only filter:

  • THB Product Form — Used to report product bugs, system issues, or request product investigations.
  • THB Marketing Form — Used for internal marketing requests and communications.
  • THB Member Support Form — Used internally for member support escalations.

These forms are not shared externally with members or case managers.

These are Humana-specific internal forms used by Case Managers and Leadership only — not for members directly.

  • Humana C3A — Referral updates, item exchanges, lost checks, emergency referrals, and account changes for C3A members.
  • Humana PHCS — Member service questions, referral address changes, provider issues, and account changes for PHCS members.
  • Humana Medicaid — Meals issues, item exchanges, referral updates, and account changes for Medicaid members.

Each team can escalate up through the tiers as needed. The standard path for all teams is Tier 1 → Tier 2 → Tier 3. Product tickets can escalate further to Tier 4 (Engineering) if dev work is required.

To escalate, update the ticket's Support Tier field in Zendesk and reassign to the appropriate group. Add a note summarizing what's been attempted and why escalation is needed.

All member grievances go to the Compliance team (Tier 2). Use the Grievances issue type on the member's form.

For UHP specifically, there is a dedicated UHP Grievance Support Form — this is Leadership only and routes directly to Governance & Risk Management at Tier 4.

🚨 Policy Update

Tier 3 Escalation Requirements

Effective immediately — all agents must follow the updated process when escalating tickets to Product Support (Tier 3).

What Changed

  • Only Ops Leads / Managers may escalate to Tier 3.
  • Tickets will only be routed to Product Support when required internal documentation is present and explicitly confirmed.
  • Tickets that arrive at Tier 3 without the required documentation will be returned to the updater at Tier 2.

Required Steps to Escalate to Tier 3

Before changing the Support Tier or assigning a ticket to Product Support, complete all of the following:

1
Add complete internal documentation — required before any escalation.
2
Add an internal/private note that includes:
  • Troubleshooting steps performed and results
  • Reproduction steps and environment details (if applicable)
  • Logs, screenshots, or attachments
  • End-user identifying information (MRN, tenant, contact, etc.)
  • Clear escalation justification — why Tier 3 is required
3
Confirm the documentation — review your internal note to ensure all required items are present before proceeding.
4
Check the field "Internal Documentation Provided for Escalation to Product Support". The ticket will only escalate when this checkbox is marked.
⚠️ Do not manually set Group → Product Support unless the documentation is already present and the confirmation checkbox is checked.

What Happens if Requirements Are Not Met

If a ticket is escalated to Tier 3 without the required documentation, the system will automatically:

  • Return the ticket to the updater (reassigned to the agent who attempted the escalation)
  • Tag the ticket missing_documentation & add_trouble_shooting_steps
  • Add an internal note describing what's missing and what actions are required
  • Product Support will not receive or work the ticket until documentation is provided

How to Remediate a Returned Ticket

1
Read the internal note that explains what's missing.
2
Add the required documentation (internal note) and attach supporting files.
3
Check "Internal Documentation Provided for Escalation to Product Support".
4
The ticket will automatically re-route to Product Support and the tags will be cleared.
